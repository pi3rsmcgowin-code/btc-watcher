❔ HELP — what do these mean?▸

Funding rate — a fee paid every 8h between traders holding BTC perpetual futures, which keeps the perp priced near real BTC. Negative = shorts pay longs = the crowd is leaning short (fearful). Positive = longs pay shorts = crowd leaning long (greedy). It's a crowd-positioning meter.

The whole idea — when funding is deeply negative, almost everyone is shorting. Crowded trades tend to reverse (shorts get squeezed), so you go long against them and also collect the negative funding as a bonus while you hold.

SIGNAL card

  • WAIT = no setup, stay flat. LONG SETUP = funding hit the bottom 5%, enter long. IN TRADE = you're holding, with time held, exit countdown, live P&L.
  • The $ number top-right is the current BTC price.

What to do in each state

  • WAIT — do nothing. Cash stays in cash, core BTC just sits. You'll be here most of the time (days at a stretch). The watcher emails you when it changes, so you can ignore it.
  • LONG SETUP NOW — your cue to act: open a long near the shown price, 1x or 2x (1x = no liquidation risk). This is when the entry email/broadcast fires.
  • IN TRADE — hold, hands off. Let the ~40h plan run; don't bail on a red number or grab profit early — the edge is in holding the full window.
  • Exit (flips back to WAIT) — close the position when the exit alert lands; reserve goes back to cash. Then you're waiting again.

Rhythm: WAIT (nothing) → LONG SETUP (enter) → IN TRADE (hold) → exit (close) → WAIT. About 2 actions per cycle, roughly once every 2 weeks, clustered in volatile stretches (~25–30/yr). Funding only updates every 8h, so setups appear on that cadence. Right now it's all paper — you're learning the cadence, not moving real money.

BTC PRICE card — last ~12 days of price. Green bands mark times funding was at/below the threshold (where the rule would go long).

FUNDING NOW vs HISTORY

  • Current funding = the live rate now. 5th pct threshold = how low it must go to trigger a long.
  • Percentile (e.g. 25th) = where funding ranks vs its own history. You want it down near the 5th (or lower).
  • Gauge / histogram: white line = funding now, green dashed line = threshold. When white sits left of green, it's crowded-short enough to trade.
